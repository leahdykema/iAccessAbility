Recommendations

Find recommended apps, products, and resources below, some of which I use on a regular basis!

Apps & Services

Magnifier - App Store: Free - A magnifier built by Apple and pre-installed.

ReBokah - App Store: Free with Optional Subscription - A magnifier developed by a visually impaired person with albinism.

Oko - App Store: Subscription Required - Hear traffic signals to cross streets safely at the right time.

Be My Eyes - App Store: Free - Call a volunteer for assistance or use AI to describe a scene.

Be My Eyes - Google Play Store: Free - Call a volunteer for assistance or use AI to describe a scene.

Seeing AI - App Store: Free - Best for reading print text.

Seeing AI - Google Play Store: Free - Best for reading print text.

Magnifier - Google Play Store: Free - A magnifier built by Google for Android.

Lookout - Google Play Store: Free - An audible visual assistant to describe your environment or read text aloud.

Noir - for iOS - App Store: $2.99 - If you prefer websites in Dark Mode, this is the perfect Safari extension for you! While there are many free extensions out there that do the same thing, Noir does it the best.

Noir - for Mac - App Store: $3.99 - If you prefer websites in Dark Mode, this is the perfect Safari extension for you! While there are many free extensions out there that do the same thing, Noir does it the best.

NVDA - NVACCESS: Free - Screen Reader

JAWS - Freedom Scientific: Subscription - Screen Reader

ZoomText - Freedom Scientific: Subscription - Display Magnifier & Screen Reader

Units - App Store: Free - The perfect visually accessible and VoiceOver compatible unit converter application with every imaginable unit of meassurement you could ever need to convert.

VisualBrailler - App Store: Free - A great way to practice Braille visually as a teacher or friend to a blind person. I mainly use this app when explaining Braille to someone or to copy and paste my Brailled name on social media!

ForScore - App Store: $24.99 with Optional Subscription - A digital sheet music library you can build and sync across devices with custom made sheet music and chord charts or import from your favorite sources.

RunGo - App Store: Free - An audible guide for runners and walkers to follow a set path.

RunGo - Google Play Store: Free - An audible guide for runners and walkers to follow a set path.

Dystopia - App Store: Free - A fully accessible third-party Reddit client for users of VoiceOver.

Products

AirPods 4 - Apple Store- $129 - Amazing sound quality at an affordable price! AirPods are a must for VoiceOver users. Not only are they comfortable and provide privacy for you, listening to your screen reader with earbuds is polite for others as well.

AirPods Pro 2 - Apple Store- $249 - If you really like your music and you are a full on Apple user, The AirPods Pro 2nd generation are perfect for you! Not only do they sync across devices seemlessly and provide incredible sound quality, but listening to VoiceOver with earbuds provides you more privacy and is more polite for others as well.

AirTag - Apple Store- $29 - Apple's item tracker is reliable and extremely helpful for keeping track of your luggage, backpack, wallet, waterbottle, and more!

Bluetooth Food Page Turner Pedal for iPad - LEKATO - Amazon: $45.99 before taxes and shipping | This pedal works wonderfully with the ForScore application mentioned above in the apps section and can be extremely useful for any musician.

Resources

AppleVis.com - An online community made up of blind and visually impaired tech savvy Apple fans helping each other out on community forums and discussions. You can also get monthly newsletters with the latest updates and new accessible apps and games!
