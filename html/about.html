My Story

Welcome to the iAccessAbility website! My name is Leah Dykema, the founder of the iAccessAbility YouTube channel, formerly known as AppleTalk.

A bit about me: I was born in the city of Xiamen City, in the provance of Fujian in China with a rare genetic condition called albinism. I spent the first 3 Â½ years of my life in an orphanage before being by a Christian American couple.. I'm the youngest of four kids. I have two older brothers who are now both married with kids and an older sister in college. Growing up in a homeschooling family, we stayed active and involved in a variety of activities. I've participated in swimming, dance, track and field, bowling, and even a Paralympic sport called Goalball. In my free time, I have enjoyed a variety of different hobbies such as cardistry, origami, and Rainbow Loom ® bracelet weeving. I currently enjoy reading, leisure writing, digital art, animating, developing my website and coding skills, and keeping up in the tech news on social media.

I've been an Apple fan, collector, and user since I was 9 years old, with the iPad and iPhone being my primary devices. Since 2017, iPads have been my essential tools for reading, writing, drawing, watching content, creating art, and so much more. My passion for technology and curiosity have driven me to become self‑taught, with YouTube and Google playing a huge role in expanding my knowledge.

My journey to independence with visual impairment has been supported by the Iowa Educational Services for the Blind and Visually Impaired (IESBVI), where I've made incredible friends and met wonderful trainers through training sessions, weekend sports retreats, and camps. I also learned Braille with the help of the Iowa Department for the Blind (IDB), and I now use it to type on my phone ‑ here's a demo of how I do that: How to Use Braille Screen Input on an iPhone or iPad - YouTube IESBVI and IDB continue to help provide training and guidance for me as I prepare for the next stage in life entering adulthood. I have learned so much with them and have met so many new people at the various camps and weekend sporting trips I have gone on through these groups.

With albinism, I am extremely sensitive to light, so I find it easiest to read with white text on a black background. Simple fonts, such as Arial or Helvetica, in larger, bold styles, are a must due to my dyslexia and visual impairment. Because I'm sensitive to light, white backgrounds can be overwhelming, often washing out content and leading to eye strain. Even small white areas, like a line or image, can reduce the visibility of the entire screen for me. But because I see light first before anything else, I see screens right away. Over the years, I've studied devices and have developed an ability to identify any iPhone and iPad at a glance, which I enjoy showing off or using to help provide help to others.

In 2018 I was asked to share weekly tips for a friend at my church. I uploaded and shared my weekly 20 to 30 minute videos through Google Drive until I created the AppleTalk YouTube channel in September of 2020. In early 2024, I rebranded to iAccessAbility and shifted my focus to sharing my knowledge of Apple accessibility as a visually impaired person with albinism. My goal is to eventually vlog about my life as a visually impaired albino while continuing to provide helpful tips, tricks, and tutorials for the blind and visually impaired community to maximize usibility of technology.

In the fall of 2024, I took an online graphic and web design class where I learned how to create websites using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. While taking that class, I began developing the iAccessAbility website and in early 2025 bought the iaccessabilityservices.com domain. The website has and will continue to be an ongoing project as I develop it and add to it in various ways to provide more resources and better accessibility for visitors.

Thank you for visiting iAccessAbility and learning a bit more about my journey. My mission is to empower individuals with visual impairments by sharing my experiences, tips, and accessibility knowledge. I hope to inspire others to embrace their unique challenges and use technology to enhance their lives. Stay connected, and feel free to reach out if you ever have questions or need guidance on navigating the world of Apple products with accessibility features. Be sure to checkout the rest of the site and the many resources around!
